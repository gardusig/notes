## 1. Bean Soup (Batch)

**Goal:** ~8–10 servings, thick, creamy, high protein, freezes well.

### Ingredients

- 1 kg black beans (or pinto)
- 3 to 3.5 liters water
- 2 medium onions, chopped
- 6 cloves garlic, minced
- 2–3 bay leaves
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- Salt and black pepper to taste

**Optional (recommended):**

- 150–300 g pork (ribs, bacon, or sausage) for flavor
- 1 carrot (adds sweetness + nutrients)
- 1 teaspoon cumin or paprika

### Instructions

1. Prep:
   - Rinse beans thoroughly.
   - (Optional) soak 8–12h → reduces cooking time.
2. Build base:
   - In pressure cooker, heat oil.
   - Sauté onion + garlic until golden.
3. Add:
   - Beans + bay leaves + optional meat/spices.
   - Add water (cover by ~3–4 cm).
4. Pressure cook:
   - High pressure → then low heat.
   - Cook 35–50 min.
5. Finish:
   - Natural pressure release.
   - Remove bay leaves.
6. Blend (key step):
   - Blend ~30–50% of the beans.
   - Return to pot → creates thick “soup” consistency.
7. Final simmer:
   - Simmer 10–15 min uncovered.
   - Adjust salt + pepper.

**Storage**

- Fridge: 4–5 days
- Freeze: up to 3 months

## 2. Vegetable + Meat Soup (7 portions)

**Goal:** daily lunch, low calorie, very high micronutrients, high satiety.

### Ingredients

**Base**

- 2.5 to 3 liters water
- 500 g lean meat (choose one):
  - ground beef (lean) OR
  - cubed beef (muscle) OR
  - turkey

**Carbs (controlled)**

- 3 medium potatoes, diced
- 1 small cassava piece (optional)

**Vegetables (high volume, low calorie)**

- 2 carrots, sliced
- 1 chayote, diced
- 1 zucchini, diced
- 1 cup cabbage, chopped
- 1 cup spinach or kale
- 1 cup broccoli (optional but great)
- 1 cup pumpkin or squash (optional)

**Fiber + extras**

- 1/2 cup peas
- 1/2 cup lentils (optional but highly recommended)

**Aromatics**

- 2 onions, chopped
- 5 cloves garlic
- 2 tomatoes, chopped
- 1 tablespoon olive oil

**Seasoning**

- Salt + black pepper
- Optional: paprika, turmeric, cumin

### Instructions

1. Base flavor:
   - Heat oil in large pot.
   - Sauté onion + garlic until fragrant.
   - Add meat and brown lightly.
2. Build structure:
   - Add tomatoes → cook until soft.
   - Add harder vegetables: potatoes, carrots, cassava, chayote.
3. Add water:
   - Pour all water.
   - Bring to boil.
4. Simmer phase 1:
   - Cook 15–20 min.
5. Add remaining:
   - zucchini, cabbage, broccoli, peas, lentils.
6. Simmer phase 2:
   - Cook another 15–20 min.
7. Final nutrients:
   - Add spinach/kale at the end (preserves nutrients).
8. Optional texture optimization:
   - Blend ~20–30% of soup.
   - Makes it thicker + more satisfying.
9. Adjust seasoning.

**Portioning**

- Divide into **7 containers**.
- ~400–500 kcal per portion (depends on meat choice).

**Why this works**

- High fiber → satiety.
- High micronutrients → recovery + health.
- Moderate protein → muscle maintenance.
- Low calorie density → easy cutting diet.

## 3. Pork + Beans

### Ingredients

- 1 kg pork
- 2 cups black beans
- 2–2.5 liters water
- 1 onion
- 3 cloves garlic
- 1–2 bay leaves
- Salt + pepper

### Instructions

1. Brown pork.
2. Add onion + garlic.
3. Pressure cook pork (40–50 min).
4. Add beans + water.
5. Pressure cook again (20–30 min).
6. Rest 30 min before serving.
