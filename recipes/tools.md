## 1. Eating setup

A **large plate with a large bowl** on top is a solid default: the plate catches drips, holds bones or peels, and insulates your hand when the bowl is hot. Eat with a **large spoon** for most things (soup, rice, beans, saucy pasta). Add a **knife** only when the food needs cutting at the table; skip it when you do not. **Forks** are optional at home; use them freely in restaurants when etiquette or the dish calls for it.

## 2. Knives

One **well-sharpened chef’s knife** is the workhorse for **picar** (dicing / chopping) **meat and vegetables** — you rarely need a full block. Add **one large serrated knife** for big fruit (pineapple, watermelon, melon) where serrated clearly wins.

## 3. Peeling

A **plastic peeler** (the can-opener-shaped kind, not a knife) is faster, safer, and wastes less on **vegetable peels / skins** than peeling with a blade. Keep one dedicated to vegetables.

## 4. Cutting board

Replace the cutting board on a **regular cadence** — surface scoring traps moisture and bacteria. Wood looks nicer but **does not clean as well**; plastic is easier to keep hygienic and cheaper to swap out.

## 5. Cookware

- **Pressure cooker** — central for **routine batch** cooking (beans, long simmers, pork + beans); one reliable pot beats juggling partial shortcuts.
- **Boiling-water pan** — dedicated pot for pasta, blanching, and large volumes of water.
- **Frying pan** — browning meat, shallow frying, finishing aromatics.
- **Large pan** — big sautés, one-pot stretches, anything that needs surface area.
- **Inox peneira + bowl for pasta** — a **stainless colander / strainer** over or into a **large bowl** drains pasta cleanly without losing shape; keeps the sink splash contained and doubles for rinsing veg when needed.

## 6. Storage boxes

Keep **a few food storage boxes** for **leftovers, pre-portioned batch, and fridge organization** — they earn their space when they reduce waste or speed weekday lunches. Decorative decanting of every dry good into matching jars is still **high-maintenance** (fill, consume, refill); skip that unless it solves a real problem (pests, humidity, measured portions).

## 7. Blender & coffee

- **Blender** — non-negotiable for the drink recipes in [`drinks.md`](drinks.md); buy enough motor for frozen fruit and leafy greens.
- **Plastic coffee support** — the **filter holder / drip basket** (or equivalent plastic frame for your brew method): cheap to replace, easy to clean, and worth keeping spare if yours cracks or stains.
