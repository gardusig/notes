# 🥘 Pressure mexidão (pernil, lagarto, liver)

Economical **red-meat pressure batch** in **cubes**: **pork pernil**, **lagarto** (lean beef), and a **modest** amount of **beef liver** for iron / B12 — then finish as **mexidão** with **rice**. Keep **salt light** during pressure; finish at the table.

## 📌 3. Prerequisites

- **Pressure cooker** (6 L+ helps for meat + later rice).
- **Cutting board + sharp knife** — **everything in ~2 cm cubes** (liver too) for even texture.
- **Wooden spoon**; **containers** for leftovers.

**Liver texture note:** liver goes **mealy** under a full tough-cut pressure cycle. **Default here:** add liver **after** the first pressure segment (see §4). If you prefer true one-pot, add liver at the start and accept softer liver.

## 📌 4. Recipe — pressure

### 🥗 Ingredients (stew base)

| Item | Weight (g) | Notes |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| Pork pernil | 500 | trimmed, cubed |
| Lagarto / lean beef | 500 | cubed |
| Beef liver | 150 | cubed (**add later** unless you chose one-pot) |
| Onion | 250 | cubed |
| Garlic | ~18 g (~6 cloves) | minced |
| Water or weak broth | ~400–600 ml | barely cover for first pressure |
| Olive oil | 2 tbsp | sauté |
| Bay, pepper | — | light salt |

### 📝 Instructions

1. Season cubes lightly with pepper (defer most salt).
2. Heat oil in pressure cooker; brown **pork + beef** in batches so cubes sear instead of steam.
3. Onion + garlic until fragrant; scrape fond.
4. Add **tomato / pepper** if using; add water to barely cover.
5. **Pressure 1:** high pressure **~25–35 min** (until beef shreds easily); natural release **~10–15 min**.
6. **Liver path A (recommended):** open; stir in **liver cubes**; **pressure 2:** **~5–8 min** — short finish so liver stays smoother.
7. **Liver path B (one-pot):** liver in step 4 with other meats — **one** pressure **~30–40 min**; texture more crumbly.
8. Taste; **salt** only at the end of stew phase.

## 📌 5. Finish as mexidão (rice)

**Option A — cooked rice (fastest)**  
Fold in **4–6 cups cooked rice** (~800–1,200 g cooked) with the stew juices; simmer open **5–10 min** to marry. Add splash of water if too tight.

**Option B — raw rice in the pot**  
Add **400–500 g dry rice** + **hot water or broth** (~1.0–1.2 L for long-grain band; adjust for your rice label). **Simmer with lid, no pressure** until rice is tender (~12–18 min for white rice; taste a grain). Stir occasionally; bottom can catch — keep heat modest.

**Servings jump** with rice — use the **with rice** row in §1 Planning.

## 📌 6. Calories and recommendations

- **Stew-only:** ~**2,200–2,800 kcal** for ~**6–8** portions · **~300–400 kcal / bowl**.
- **With rice finish:** total pot often **~3,800–4,800 kcal** for **8–12** plates · **~350–450 kcal / plate** when divided wider.

**Health framing**

- **Liver** is nutrient-dense; keep the share **small** so the meal stays mostly lean muscle meat.
- **Salt discipline:** pressure + reduced sauces **amplify** salt; undershoot during cooking.
