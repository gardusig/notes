## 📌 3. Prerequisites

**Vessel and water**

- **Mug or cup** under the drip path.
- **Hot water** just off boil (kettle, thermos pour, or inline heater).

**Coffee and filter path**

- **Ground coffee** for filter brewing; airtight **storage** at home.
- **Paper filter** matched to your **holder** geometry (basket / #2 / #4).
- **Filter holder** on the mug (or small stand).
- **Dosing:** scoop or small scale for repeatability.
- **Plastic filter frame / drip basket** — spare when it cracks or stains.

---

## 📌 4. Recipe

### 🥗 Ingredients (per cup)

- Ground coffee for filter (amount by taste; same scoop or scale each time)
- Hot water to target volume

### 📝 Instructions

1. Seat the **paper filter in the holder over the mug**.
2. Add coffee; optionally **bloom** with a little water, wait a few seconds.
3. Pour the rest in stages to your target volume; let drain.
4. Discard filter and grounds; rinse the holder if needed.

## 📌 5. Calories and recommendations

- **Black coffee** is effectively **~0 kcal** per cup; milk, sugar, or syrup stack on top.

**Caffeine (daily budget)**

- **Small:** about **~60–90 mg** — ~1–2 tsp instant in one drink **or** one short filter cup.
- **Medium:** about **~100–150 mg** — stronger instant **or** one typical mug of filter at your usual grams.
- **Daily cap:** aim for **at most ~3 small** *or* **at most ~2 medium** from **all** sources (coffee, tea, cola, pre-workout), not both limits maxed the same day.
- **Sleep:** soft cutoff **late afternoon** if sleep is sensitive.

- **Grind + filter size:** mismatched filters tear or bypass; buy the box that matches your holder.
