# Online feeds — habit runbook

How to use **live and algorithmic feeds** (YouTube, Twitch, broadcast streams) as a **habit**, not as a substitute for logged film/TV. For watched titles and queues, see **[media/README.md](../media/README.md)**. For **concrete Twitch/live follows**, see **[live-channels.md](live-channels.md)**.

**Upstream:** [consume/README.md](../README.md)

---

## IRL reset

When you feel **too far from reality**, bias toward **what is happening now**: live streams (Twitch, YouTube Live), esports broadcasts (for example regional leagues or international events), or speedrun marathons. Treat them as a **time anchor** and a sample of current culture—not as your default “always on” entertainment. Pair with real-world contact or errands when possible so the feed is not the only tether.

## Subscriptions

- **Prune on purpose.** Many subscriptions are from earlier phases; they shape the home feed whether you watch them or not.
- **Add when there is a recurring need**, not on impulse mid-scroll.
- **Review removals** on a simple cadence (calendar or recurring task): if a channel has not earned a slot in months, unsubscribe or move it to a list outside the algorithm (saved playlist, note, “investigate later” in Keep).
- **Defer investigation:** if something might be useful later, capture the name/link for later search instead of subscribing immediately.

## Background feed policy

- **Ambient use is allowed** when the goal is company, texture, or low-focus stimulation—not when you need deep work unless you have tested that pairing.
- **Match audio to the moment:** sometimes voices help; sometimes music-only or visuals-only works better. Switch instead of tolerating friction.
- **Avoid random entry:** prefer **Watch Later**, playlists, or a chosen live channel over “first thumbnail that loads.”
- **If it truly matters**, wait a few days and watch deliberately; do not treat urgency in the feed as a moral emergency.
- **Movies** are a poor default for this lane (heavy commitment, less “habit” signal). **TV series** (especially rewatches) can support **flow** and comfort; keep long-form fiction mostly in **[media/README.md](../media/README.md)**.

## English vs PT/ES stream

Split **by job**, not by snobbery:

| Lane | Role | Examples (illustrative, not exhaustive) |
|------|------|-------------------------------------------|
| **English** | Professional density: programming depth, TV-style business news, documentary or “slow TV” comfort in a language you use for work | Tech/education streamers; networks such as CNBC or CNN for headlines; calm factual series (pawn/consignment formats, myth-busting science, nature docs) |
| **Portuguese / Spanish** | Humor, cultural comfort, dubbed content with commentary, food or lifestyle where the language is part of the relaxation | Comedy and reaction formats; chefs; “boat life” or slow domestic vlogs where tone matters more than jargon |

**Entertainment that crosses languages:** sports finals, gaming events, speedruns—pick the commentary track that keeps you grounded, not the one that pulls you into rage-bait.

## Deny list (ambient feed)

These patterns are **not recommended as always-on or idle background**. If you choose them, treat that as **intentional viewing** (seated, bounded session), same rule as below for high-risk categories.

**Intentional viewing only** (never ambient default):

- **Gambling and gambling-adjacent** mechanics (loot hype, “opening,” odds language framed as entertainment).
- **Recreational intoxication or substance glamor** (party aesthetics, consumption as personality).

**Avoid as ambient feed** (neutral pattern labels):

- **Wealth flex / finance lifestyle** — assumes spending or income you are not optimizing for right now.
- **Food outside your diet goals** — triggers grazing mindset; recipes belong in deliberate cooking sessions.
- **Appearance-first content** — high distraction, low information for your current priorities.
- **Purchase-forward hauls and “shop with me”** — when you need an item, search that item; useful recommendations surface without following haul channels.
- **Music discovery as feed** — use playlists or intentional search.
- **Video game discovery as feed** — use **[play/README.md](../../play/README.md)** queues and search.
- **Travel explorer filler** — when you want a place, search that place on purpose.
- **Startup hustle and quick-win framing** — if you ever start something, prefer a planned process over feed-born urgency.
- **Relationship vlog formats** — parasocial pull with little actionable return.
- **Sports or hobbies you cannot participate in** — envy spiral risk as background.
- **Politics and luxury consumption** — high arousal, low control.
- **Automotive spending content** — aligns attention with spending, not earning.

Phrasing is about **categories**, not individuals.

## Seek list

What to **actively** hunt when you open the app on purpose:

- **Random curiosity and general knowledge** — odd documentaries, explainers, one-off deep dives.
- **Humor** — in the language lane that actually relaxes you.
- **News** — bounded session with an exit time, not an infinite sidebar.

**Heuristics:**

- **YouTube works best when you already know the target** (channel, playlist, or search string). The recommendation bubble is hard to escape once you are inside it.
- **Live** can answer “what are people doing **now**?” for ideas and cultural temperature—**optional**, not mandatory homework.

## Emergency calm content

When you need **regulation** (anxiety spike, insomnia spiral, “too much internet brain”):

- **Traditional broadcast-style TV** over pure algorithm: Brazilian free-to-air examples include **SBT**, **Globo**, **Record** (linear predictability, less infinite scroll). **Telenovelas** can be slow, legible drama—use when that predictability is soothing.
- **Low-arousal visuals:** cleaning and restoration (water, dirt removal, before/after), **satisfying completions** (tight edits, clear end state), **construction and materials** (concrete, clay, woodworking—texture without plot twist).
- **Slow domestic or artisan tasks on screen:** vegetable prep, house cleaning, patient handwork—audio-off or low narration as needed.

Keep a **small saved list** (playlist or note) of three to five go-tos so you do not have to search while dysregulated.
