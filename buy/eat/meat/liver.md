# 🍳 Liver — pan cook (milder flavor)

**Beef or chicken liver** seared **hot and fast** with **onions** (and optional **bacon** depth). The usual “bad” notes are **metallic / bitter / chalky** — almost always **overcooking**, **untrimmed membrane**, or **bile / damaged spots** left on the meat. This lane is about **prep + time control**, not masking with heavy spice.

## 📌 2. Why liver tastes “off”

| Cause | What to do |
| --- | --- |
| **Overcooked** (gray all through) | **Pull early**: thin slices → **seconds–90 s per side** at high heat; aim **blush / barely pink** inside for **beef** if you tolerate it; **chicken** should hit **no raw** but still **yielding**—go by firmness, not color alone. |
| **Membrane / silverskin** left on | **Trim** the tough film; **slice** uniform thickness so nothing steams unevenly. |
| **Bile or damaged patches** (green / dark spots) | **Cut away** those bits entirely — they read as **bitter** no matter what else you do. |
| **Blood / purge liquid** on the surface | **Rinse** quickly if the pack smells sharp; **pat very dry** before the pan or you **steam** instead of **sear**. |
| **Strong species note** (beef > chicken) | Optional **soak** 30–120 min: **milk** or **buttermilk** (mild), or **cold salted water** (rinse after). Not mandatory if prep + doneness are right. |

**Pan rule:** **crowding = gray + steam = bad flavor**. Cook in **batches** if needed.

## 📌 4. Prerequisites

- **Sharp knife** + board — trimming and **even slices** (~8–12 mm for beef; slightly thicker for chicken if you like).
- **Heavy skillet**; **vent** (smoke on high heat).
- **Paper towels** — surface **dry** before sear.

---

## 📌 5. Recipe — onions + sear

### 📌 Prep

1. **Inspect liver**: remove **membrane**, **fat with gristle**, any **green / off** patches.
2. **Optional soak:** cover slices in **milk** 30–120 min in the fridge → **drain** → **pat dry**.
3. **Slice** to even thickness; **pepper** lightly; **salt** right before cooking (or a minute before) so less moisture weeps out.

### 📌 Cook

1. If using **bacon**: render in the dry pan until crisp → **remove** bacon bits; leave **fat** for flavor.
2. **Caramelize onions** in butter/oil/bacon fat on **medium** until soft and sweet (~10–15 min). **Push onions aside** or **remove** to a plate.
3. **Crank heat**; add a thin **high-smoke oil** film if the pan looks dry.
4. **Sear liver in one layer** without crowding: **~45–90 s per side** for thin beef slices (adjust for thickness); chicken often **slightly less**. You want **crust** outside, **not gray all the way through**.
5. **Optional:** return onions (and bacon) to the pan; toss **off heat** 10–20 s with a **knob of butter** if you want a gloss.
6. **Rest** 1–2 min on a warm plate; **lemon** squeeze + **parsley** at the table.

**If it still tastes strong:** next time **shorter sear**, **milk soak**, or **smaller portion** mixed into rice / mexidão instead of a straight liver plate.
