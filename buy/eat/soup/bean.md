## 📌 3. Prerequisites

**Cookware**

- **Pressure cooker (~6 L+)** — **required** (electric or stovetop). This bean runbook is written around pressure from **dry** beans; do not substitute a plain stockpot for the same times.
- **Wooden spoon** or heat-safe spatula.

**Cutting**

- **Cutting board** and **chef’s knife** for onion, garlic, optional carrot.

**Texture finish**

- **Countertop blender** or **immersion blender** to blend ~30–50% of the beans after pressure (key thickness step).

**Storage**

- **Food storage boxes** for fridge or freezer portions.

## 📌 4. Recipe

**Goal:** ~8–10 servings, thick, creamy, high protein, freezes well.

### 🥗 Ingredients

| Item | Type | Qty | Weight (g) | kcal (approx.) |
| --- | --- | --- | ---: | ---: |
| Black or pinto beans (dry) | legume | 1 kg | 1,000 | ~3,320 |
| Water | liquid | 3–3.5 L | 3,250 | 0 |
| Onion (medium, chopped) | aromatic | 2 | 250 | ~100 |
| Garlic (minced) | aromatic | 6 cloves | 18 | ~27 |
| Bay leaves | seasoning | 2–3 | 1 | ~0 |
| Olive oil | fat | 2 tbsp | 28 | ~240 |
| Salt, black pepper | seasoning | to taste | — | ~0 |
| Pork (ribs, bacon, or sausage) — *optional* | protein | 225 g | 225 | ~270 |
| Carrot — *optional* | vegetable | 1 medium | 60 | ~25 |

### 📝 Instructions

1. Prep:
   - Rinse beans thoroughly.
   - (Optional) soak 8–12h → reduces cooking time.
2. Build base:
   - In pressure cooker, heat oil.
   - Sauté onion + garlic until golden.
3. Add:
   - Beans + bay leaves + optional meat/spices.
   - Add water (cover by ~3–4 cm).
4. Pressure cook:
   - High pressure → then low heat.
   - Cook 35–50 min.
5. Finish:
   - Natural pressure release.
   - Remove bay leaves.
6. Blend (key step):
   - Blend ~30–50% of the beans.
   - Return to pot → thick “soup” consistency.
7. Final simmer:
   - Simmer 10–15 min uncovered.
   - Adjust salt + pepper.

**Storage**

- Fridge: 4–5 days
- Freeze: up to 3 months
