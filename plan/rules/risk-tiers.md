# Risk tiers (A–E)

**Recommendation ladder:** higher tiers override lower ones. When something is new, **plan it** against this file plus [`../../README.md#current-snapshot`](../../README.md#current-snapshot) and your own long-arc notes (outside this repo if you keep them private).

**Not legal advice.** Prefer choices you could **explain to future-you** without relying on secrecy.

**Upstream:** [Rules hub](README.md)

## Realism about risk

- Low probability is not zero; “it won’t happen to me” is not a plan.  
- If something goes wrong, consequences are often **clear and expensive** (legal, social, health, money).  
- Delayed or fuzzy outcomes still **count** for stress and memory — “accountability fuzzy” often feeds anxiety afterward.

---

## Tier A — Do not do (at all costs)

**Universal hard no (bad anywhere, regardless of mood or context)**

- Violence, coercion, non-consensual acts, credible threats  
- Theft, fraud, deliberate destruction of what isn’t yours  
- Severe self-harm; irreversible acts while judgment is impaired  
- Anything that clearly trades long-term freedom or health for a short spike  

If it would be wrong on the front page of a newspaper in any country, treat it as **Tier A**.

---

## Tier B — Strongly not recommended (places, patterns, costs)

**Places / situations often poor value for peace of mind** (risk–stress lens, not a travel brochure)

- **Active war zones** and areas with collapsing public order → extreme unpredictability; help is thin.  
- **Asia (snapshot)** — **Japan:** often safe by violent-crime measures; friction can be **social, language, and rule-following**. **Singapore:** **strict laws and stiff penalties** for many offenses visitors sometimes underestimate. **China:** surveillance and legal norms differ from many Western defaults — assume **compliance and documentation** matter. **Russia:** geopolitical and legal volatility. **North Korea:** treat as **do not go / do not engage** for almost any personal rationale. Mistakes or misunderstandings can carry **outsized** consequences — plan conservatively.  
- **Europe (snapshot)** — **UK** and **Ireland:** broadly familiar rule-of-law patterns to many travelers, but **still** jurisdiction-specific (speech, transit, local conduct rules, visas). **Europe overall:** not uniform — **check the specific country**; **no place removes Tier A**.  
- **Middle East** — not one place; **research the specific country and current conflict/advisories**; avoid hot borders / active conflict without a sober plan.

**Environment / nature**

- **Oceania (e.g. Australia)** — heat, remote distances, and dangerous fauna are real plan items.  
- **Hot climates** — dehydration, sun, and fatigue change judgment fast; budget water, shade, and pace.  
- **High-friction adventure** (remote ferries, tight schedules) — wonderful if planned, stressful if improvised when tired.

**Patterns**

- Repeated **financial cliff-edges** (debt tricks, scams, “sure thing” leverage)  
- **Escalation hobbies** where one mistake is permanent  

---

## Tier C — Not recommended (regret, reputation, spiral risk)

- Gossip-as-weapon and public humiliation campaigns  
- Edge-of-law “gray” schemes where upside is small and tail risk is large  
- Burning bridges when alternatives exist  

---

## Tier D — May be legal or common, but high emotional residue (personal caution)

Not an “evil tier” — **high conflict potential** or **heavy rumination** for some people.

- **Reproductive choices** — legally and emotionally **jurisdiction-dependent**; often high load; decide with **time, facts, and support**, not impulse.  
- **Killing animals** (even “small” or “pests”) — can leave lasting imagery and moral weight; prefer **non-lethal / professional / delegated** handling when possible.  
- **Deliberately offending people with words** — cheap in the moment, expensive in reputation and rumination; prefer **clear boundaries** without seeking hurt.  

If you already run anxious, treat Tier D as **“schedule a walk before you decide.”**

---

## Tier E — Repeat vs start

**Usually fine to repeat** (when history says low harm)

- Routines that did not produce legal, health, financial, or relationship damage  
- Low-stakes comforts that **stay bounded** (align with [`../../README.md#current-snapshot`](../../README.md#current-snapshot) money reality)  

**Often not worth starting now**

- **Pets** and **expensive luxury** while money is tight — fixed costs + attention compound stress.  
- **New complexity** during instability (moves, debt, burnout) — see [decisions.md#delayed-decisions-timing-strategy](decisions.md#delayed-decisions-timing-strategy).  
- **Activities that load an injury** you are actively managing — prefer neutral loading and validate with a clinician if unsure.  

**May be good to start** (bounded tools)

- **Video games** and **musical instruments** — can be cheap at entry, stoppable, and contained; still use **budget caps** so they do not become Tier C financially.
