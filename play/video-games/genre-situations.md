## Quick map — situation → reach for

| Situation | Good genre / vibe | Often a poor fit |
|-----------|-------------------|------------------|
| **Wired at night, cold room, not ready to sleep** | Survival horror, tense thrillers—lean into atmosphere; keep volume/sessions bounded so you can still wind down. | Long cozy sims that blur into “one more hour.” |
| **High energy, want laughs, short wins** | Snappy 2D comedy, arcade-style runs, light platformers—**not** the same as brutal precision runs unless you *want* that spike (see **Boss rush** below). | Slow 4X or grindy MMO loops unless that *is* the mood. |
| **Need humility + proof you can improve** | Boss rush / precision / **soulslike**—repeatable lessons from failure. | Completionist LEGO when you only have 20 minutes. |
| **Want checklist progress without stress** | Licensed collectathon / **LEGO**-style checklist maps—clear bars, low narrative stakes. | Horror or PvP where outcomes feel random. |
| **Multi-day strategic headspace** | **4X / grand strategy**—long arcs, inter-session planning. | Anything that demands twitch reflexes before coffee. |
| **Want “job fantasy” from the couch** | **Simulators** (truck, shop, flight)—rhythm + systems + optional wheel/chair rig. | Narrative-heavy RPGs if you want story beats every session. |
| **Crave exploration + light puzzle friction** | Open-world **exploration + puzzle** (e.g. **Assassin’s Creed**-style parkour + tombs). | Pure fighting-game ladder if exploration is the itch. |

## Boss rush / precision / soulslike

These games **compress feedback**: you fail visibly, adjust, and earn the next inch of progress. They reward **patience and execution** more than luck. They can **ground ego** because the game does not care about your resume—only whether you learned the pattern.

**When to choose it:** you want **skill growth you can feel** and can tolerate **dozens of attempts** on the same gate.

*Examples from your history:* [Cuphead](completed/2d.md); queue: **Dark Souls II** in [owned-queue.md](owned-queue.md).

---

## Licensed collectathon / LEGO-style checklist

Straightforward **loops**, **collectibles**, and **humor**—good when you want **structure** without emotional heavy lifting. **100%** can be its own satisfying project when you like **clear completion bars**.

**When to choose it:** you want **predictable fun** and **checklist momentum**, especially with **limited mental bandwidth**.

*Examples from your history:* several **LEGO** titles in [completed/3d.md](completed/3d.md).

---

## 4X / grand strategy

**Long horizons**: tech trees, diplomacy, and “where will I be in ten turns?” thinking. Matches **spread across days** feel natural.

**When to choose it:** you have **multi-hour or multi-session** windows and enjoy **planning more than moment-to-moment reflexes**.

*Queue:* add a title to [owned-queue.md](owned-queue.md) or [to-buy.md](to-buy.md) when you pick one up.

---

## Simulators (“job fantasy”)

**Systems-heavy** experiences—routes, physics, schedules—played for **calm focus** or **role-play**. A good chair and wheel can turn truck sim into **physical** play, not just screen time.

**When to choose it:** you want **immersion in a repeatable job** without real-world stakes.

---

## Open-world exploration + puzzle

**Traversal fantasy** plus **environmental puzzles** or stealth puzzles—good when you want **wonder** and **mild friction** (not a pure boss gate).

**When to choose it:** you want to **wander**, **climb**, and **solve** without committing to a short brutal loop.

*Example in queue:* **Assassin’s Creed Unity** in [owned-queue.md](owned-queue.md).

---

## Survival / psychological horror

Horror is often **best when the room matches the fiction**: **night**, **quiet house**, **cool air**, headphones—**presence** over distraction. If you are **trying to fix insomnia**, treat horror like **caffeine**: sometimes it **settles** the brain via adrenaline; sometimes it **delays** sleep—**pick short sessions** and know your pattern.

**When to choose it:** you want **tension as entertainment** and can **stop on a save** without chasing “one more chapter.”

*Example in queue:* **Resident Evil 0** in [owned-queue.md](owned-queue.md).

---

## Light / comic / short-session 2D

**Jokes, bright art, low stakes**—great when you are **already up** and want **playfulness** without a huge narrative commitment.

**When to choose it:** you are **enthusiastic** and want **quick dopamine** or **co-op banter**.

*Examples in queue:* **Hidden Folks**, **Mini Metro** in [owned-queue.md](owned-queue.md).
