# 🐟 Sardine, egg & rice pan (everyday lane)

Brazilian **mexidão-style** one-pan meal: **canned sardine** first, **eggs** folded in, then **rice** cooked in the same pot with the fond and oil. Keep **sardine cans, eggs, and rice** at home for a **~20–30 min** plate when you are in a hurry—not a batch stew like [meat mexidão](meat/mexidao.md).

## 📌 3. Prerequisites

- **Wide pan or shallow pot with lid** (rice needs a lid for the simmer phase).
- **Wooden spoon**; bowl to beat eggs.
- **Can opener**; drain sardines (reserve a little oil from the can only if you want extra fish flavor).

**Stock at home:** at least **2–3 sardine cans**, **½–1 kg rice**, **6+ eggs** so this lane is always one shop away.

## 📌 4. Recipe — one pan

### 🥗 Ingredients (default single pan)

| Item | Amount | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Sardine (canned) | 2–3 cans (~125 g each) | drain; break into chunks |
| Eggs | 4–6 | beat lightly with a pinch of salt |
| White rice (dry) | 300–400 g | rinsed if your habit |
| Water or weak broth | ~600–750 ml | ~2× rice volume; adjust to pack |
| Oil | 2 tbsp | start sauté |
| Onion / garlic | optional | small dice |

### 📝 Instructions

1. **Sardine base** — Heat oil. Optional: onion and garlic until soft. Add **sardine**; cook **2–4 min**, breaking fish apart and letting moisture cook off (do not mash to paste).
2. **Eggs** — Push fish to the side or mix through. Pour in **beaten eggs**; stir gently until **just set** (soft scramble), **1–2 min**.
3. **Rice in the same pan** — Stir in **dry rice** so grains coat in oil and fond. Add **hot water/broth** and a **light** pinch of salt. **Lid on**, **medium-low simmer** **~12–18 min** until rice is tender and liquid absorbed (follow your rice pack; peek once near the end).
4. **Rest** — Off heat, lid on, **5 min**. Fluff with a fork. Adjust salt; **lemon wedge** or black pepper at the table if you like.

**Faster variant (pre-cooked rice):** after step 2, fold in **~600–800 g cooked rice** and a splash of water; lid on **5–8 min** to heat through—skips the raw-rice simmer but uses the same pan story.

## 📌 5. Calories and recommendations

- **~1,800–2,400 kcal** full pan · **~450–650 kcal / plate** at 3–4 servings.
- **Protein-forward** for a cheap pantry cook: fish + eggs + starch in one sitting.

**When to use**

- **Hurry nights** — minimal prep, one dish, no pressure cooker.
- **Not** a substitute for [meat mexidão](meat/mexidao.md) when you want a **large meat batch** for the week.
