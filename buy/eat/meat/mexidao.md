# 🇧🇷 Mexidão — cheap varied pork & beef (pressure batch)

Brazilian-style **mexidão**: one cook that **mixes several inexpensive red-meat cuts** for depth of flavor — **pork and beef only** (no **chicken**, no **fish**). Pressure (or long simmer) in **cubes**, then finish as a thick **mexidão** with **white rice** *or* with **cassava** (mandioca / aipim). Keep **salt light** under pressure; finish salt at the table.

## 📌 3. Prerequisites

- **Pressure cooker** (6 L+ helps for meat + optional rice finish).
- **Cutting board + sharp knife** — **~2 cm cubes** on muscle meats; liver same for even cooking.
- **Wooden spoon**; **containers** for leftovers.
- **Vegetable peeler** if doing cassava same day.

**Liver:** still **mealy** if pressured as long as tough cuts — **default:** add liver **after** the first pressure segment (§4).

## 📌 4. Recipe — pressure

### 🥗 Ingredients (stew base) — scale to your §2 mix

| Item | Weight (g) | Notes |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| Pork (pernil / paleta + optional bacon) | ~500–650 | trimmed, cubed |
| Beef (lagarto / patinho / músculo / acém) | ~400–600 | cubed |
| Beef liver | 100–200 | cubed (**add later** unless one-pot) |
| Onion | ~250 | cubed |
| Garlic | ~18 g (~6 cloves) | minced |
| Water or weak broth | ~400–600 ml | barely cover for first pressure |
| Olive oil | 2 tbsp | sauté |
| Bay, pepper | — | light salt |

### 📝 Instructions

1. Season cubes lightly with pepper (defer most salt).
2. Heat oil; brown **pork + beef** in batches so cubes **sear** instead of steam. If using **bacon / toucinho**, render first, then brown other meats.
3. Onion + garlic until fragrant; scrape fond.
4. Optional tomato / pepper; add water to barely cover.
5. **Pressure 1:** high **~25–35 min** until beef shreds easily; natural release **~10–15 min**.
6. **Liver (recommended):** open; stir in **liver**; **Pressure 2:** **~5–8 min**.
7. **One-pot liver:** liver from step 4 — single **~30–40 min** pressure; liver more crumbly.
8. Taste; **salt** at end of stew phase.

## 📌 5. Finish — rice or cassava

### 📌 A) Rice mexidão

**Cooked rice (fastest)** — fold **4–6 cups cooked rice** (~800–1,200 g cooked) into stew; simmer open **5–10 min**; splash of water if tight.

**Raw rice in the pot** — **400–500 g dry rice** + **hot water/broth** (~1.0–1.2 L for typical white rice; follow pack). **Lid on, simmer, no pressure** until tender (~12–18 min); modest heat; stir occasionally.

### 📌 B) Cassava (mandioca) mexidão

**Parallel boil (simplest)** — peel cassava; remove **fibrous core**; chunk. **Boil salted water** until fork-tender (~20–35 min depending on chunk size). Drain; fold chunks into finished stew; simmer open **5–10 min** so roots drink the sauce.

**One-pot (more care)** — add **peeled chunks** after Pressure 1 (before liver if using two-stage liver): barely cover with **hot** water/broth; **simmer with lid** until cassava is tender, then continue liver step if needed. Stir often — cassava **sticks** easier than rice.

## 📌 6. Calories and recommendations

- **Stew-only:** ~**2,200–2,800 kcal** · **~6–8** bowls · **~300–400 kcal / bowl**.
- **+ rice:** often **~3,800–4,800 kcal** · **8–12** plates.
- **+ cassava:** often **~3,400–4,400 kcal** · **7–10** plates (cassava is dense starch).

**Health framing**

- **Liver** stays a **small** fraction so the meal is still mostly muscle meat.
- **Salt:** pressure + reduction **amplify** salt — undershoot while cooking.

**Premium nights**

- For **charcoal steak** or **frigideira filé**, see [barbecue.md](barbecue.md) and [frigideira.md](frigideira.md).
