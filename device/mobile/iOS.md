# iPhone — secure home phone (emergency, recovery & reinvest)

## Purpose

This note is for the **smallest / cheapest current iPhone** that still meets a **security** bar: **ongoing iOS updates**, **hardware encryption**, **Face ID** or **Touch ID** + **strong passcode**, **Find My** / **Activation Lock**, and **iCloud** backup.

Treat it as a **home phone** on **Wi‑Fi**, not a pocket flagship: **emergencies** (identity, safety, reaching family), **recovery** (lost street phone, locked accounts, gov.br and bank flows from a **clean** device), and **reinvest** (statements, **Tesouro**, brokerages, **MEI** / tax chores **without** doing that work on the **Android** street handset).

**vs Android:** **[android.md](./android.md)** stays the **street** lane (stricter **Wallet**, fewer bank apps). This iPhone can hold **more** institutions, **fuller** **Google productivity**, and **most** **federal** citizen apps you only need in a calm, private setting. Folder outline: **[../README.md](../README.md)**.

---

## Summary

- **Hardware goal:** **Least expensive** iPhone still sold with **current** iOS support—often the **smallest screen** in the lineup (e.g. **SE-class** or **base-number** model, depending on Apple’s active range). Prefer **new** or **Apple refurb** for predictable battery; **used** is OK if you check **battery health** and **FMi** status.
- **Software:** Stay on **latest iOS**; enable **Find My**, **iCloud Backup**, **Face ID** / **Touch ID**, **passcode**.
- **Use pattern:** **Wi‑Fi at home** for installs, backups, long reads, and **sensitive** flows; keep **scrolling / delivery / transit** on **Android** when possible.
- **Battery (ballpark):** For **this guide’s use** (mostly **Wi‑Fi**, short sessions), many **standard** (non-Pro) iPhones with a **healthy** cell go **~2–4 days** between charges; **SE / compact** tiers are often **~1–2 days**. If you use **cellular** all day like a primary phone, expect closer to **~5–8 h screen-on** / **daily** charge—see **[Battery life (rough estimates)](#battery-life-rough-estimates)**.

## Setup & installation

### Initial setup

#### Basic configuration

1. Connect to Wi-Fi
2. Sign in with your Apple ID
3. Update system

**Settings → General → Software Update**

Do **not** rely on this device for connectivity outside the home unless you add a SIM later—treat **Wi‑Fi at home** as the normal path for updates, backups, and app installs.

#### Security

**Settings → Face ID & Passcode** (or **Touch ID & Passcode** on models with a Home button)

- Enable **Face ID** or **Touch ID**
- Set **Passcode**
- Enable **Require Passcode** as soon as practical (e.g. **Immediately**)

#### Backup

**Settings → Apple ID → iCloud → iCloud Backup**

- Enable automatic backup
- Ensures recovery of apps, photos, and settings

### Apple ID & services

Your Apple ID enables:

- App Store (app installation)
- iCloud backup & restore
- Contacts sync
- Calendar sync
- Photos backup (iCloud Photos)
- Device tracking (Find My)

#### Find My — if the iPhone is lost, stolen, or left behind

Turn it on **before** something happens; **Activation Lock** (anti-theft) depends on this.

1. **Settings →** your name **→ Find My → Find My iPhone →** enable **Find My iPhone**.
2. Enable **Find My network** if you want other Apple devices to help locate this iPhone when it is offline (battery dead or no Wi‑Fi/cellular), when Apple supports it in your region.
3. Enable **Send Last Location** so the phone can report its last position before the battery runs out.

**If the iPhone disappears**

1. On **another Apple device**, open the **Find My** app → **Devices** → select the iPhone → **Play Sound**, **Lost Mode**, or **Erase This Device** as needed.
2. Or in any browser go to **[icloud.com/find](https://www.icloud.com/find)** and sign in with the **same Apple ID**.
3. **Lost Mode** locks the screen, shows a short message and a callback number, and suspends cards in **Apple Wallet** (where supported)—use it before erasing if you still hope to recover the phone.
4. **Erase iPhone** is last resort: it removes your data and disables the device until someone signs in with your Apple ID (Activation Lock). Only erase if you accept it may not come back.
5. File a **police report** if it was stolen; note **serial number** (**Settings → General → About**) when you still have the device, or from the box / purchase receipt.

### Banking & payments

Banks, wallets, and money movement (separate from the Google product suite).

#### Policy (home phone)

- **Number of bank apps:** **No fixed cap**—install **backup** institutions, **broker** logins you do **not** want on the street phone, and anything you need after **losing** or **wiping** the Android.
- **Per bank / per wallet:** configure **outgoing limits**, **alerts**, and **per-transaction** rules so huge moves are not one tap. Split large transfers across **days** or **desktop / branch** when possible.
- **Risk posture:** **Less exposed in public** than Android, but **theft** and **account takeover** still count—treat limits as mandatory.

## What to buy & price expectations

### Smallest / cheapest secure iPhone

Aim for the **lowest-price new iPhone** that still receives **the current major iOS** (check [apple.com](https://www.apple.com) and **compare** pages). Typical pattern:

- **iPhone SE** generations: **Touch ID**, compact, **lower display quality** than flagships—often the **cheapest** entry with full **Secure Enclave** behavior.
- **Standard** (non-Pro) **number** models: **Face ID**, larger screen, better cameras—**step up** if SE is discontinued or only slightly cheaper.

**Security checklist (non-negotiable):**

- Device can run **current** iOS (not stuck on an old major with no patches).
- **Find My** and **Activation Lock** usable with your Apple ID.
- **Biometrics** + **strong passcode** enabled day one.
- Prefer **Apple** or **authorized** refurb for **battery** and **warranty**; if **used**, verify **Settings → General → About** and that the device is **not** activation-locked to someone else’s account.

### Battery life (rough estimates)

This phone is framed as **home / backup**, not all-day **cellular**—so **Wi‑Fi–biased** numbers come first. Treat all figures as **rough**; check **Settings → Battery → Battery Health** (and **Maximum Capacity** %) on **used** units.

| Role | Typical pattern | Typical range (healthy battery) |
| ---- | ----------------- | ------------------------------- |
| **As in this guide** | Mostly **Wi‑Fi**, banking / gov apps, 2FA, short bursts—**no** long gaming or video | **Standard** (non-Pro) **number** models: often **~2–4 days** between charges; **SE** / smaller batteries: often **~1–2 days** |
| **Light daily carry** | Some **cellular** + camera + messaging, modest screen time | **~1–2 days** for most current **non-Pro** iPhones; **SE** closer to **~1 day** |
| **Primary-phone style** | Bright screen, **5G**, navigation, streaming on data | Often **~5–9 h** screen-on over a day, i.e. **nightly charge** for many models; **smaller** tiers trend **lower** |

**Aging:** Below **~80%** **Maximum Capacity**, real endurance can drop **noticeably**—**Apple** (or quality third-party) **battery replacement** restores headroom.

### Storage and longevity

- **128 GB** is a practical floor for apps + photos + offline docs; **64 GB** can work if you stay lean and use **iCloud**.
- **AppleCare+** is optional; for a **budget backup** phone, a **good case** and **regular backups** matter more than max hardware tier.

### Money

- Compare **Apple refurb**, **carrier** (if you ever add a line), and **retail** promos. **Prices move**—this note does not pin SKUs by generation.
