# Puzzles — runbook

**This folder is for:** activities that reward **patience** and **quiet brain work**—logic, pattern-finding, and steady focus. They stimulate the mind without the same “session energy” as **[video-games/](../video-games/README.md)**.

**Default medium:** **Physical**—real pieces, paper, pen, a board. **Apps** on phone or laptop are allowed when practical, but the experience is usually **less satisfying** than the tangible version; treat digital as a fallback, not the main goal.

**Requirements:** Each format below can list what you need (space, gear, posture). Add stricter notes here as you discover what actually works for you.

## Jigsaw puzzles

**Aim:** slow, visual patience work with a **portable** way to **sort and lay out pieces** (trays, roll-up mat, folding board, etc.) so you are not tied to one huge permanent table.

**Surface:** Any **stable, flat** support works. **In bed** is tempting but easy to strain **neck/back**; **sitting on the floor** (or a low table with a straight back) is usually **more sustainable** for longer sessions.

## Printed puzzles

**Aim:** puzzles on **paper**—from a **magazine**, **newspaper**, or **something you print** at home. Examples: **logic** puzzles, **sudoku**, **crossword**, **cryptic crossword**.

**Typical needs:** printer (if you supply the PDF/page), paper, pen or pencil, good light, a flat writing surface. Easier on posture than hunching over a phone.

---

## Apps (optional)

Phone or laptop apps are fine for travel or a quick hit, but they sit **second** to physical play when you care about the **feel** of the activity and a calmer headspace.

## How to use this folder

1. **Prefer physical** when you have time and space; note **requirements** (gear, surface, posture) in the sections above as you refine them.
2. **Rhythm:** Optional “daily” vs “weekend only” so this doesn’t compete with video games or instruments.
3. **Video puzzle games:** If it’s really a **game session** (progression, action), **[video-games/](../video-games/README.md)** may fit better; keep this folder for **slow, patient** puzzle time.
