# 2D games

**Legend**

- **HLTB main / HLTB 100%:** rough community-style times (main story vs completionist). Confirm on [HowLongToBeat](https://howlongtobeat.com/)—numbers move as more people submit.
- **Difficulty:** coarse **typical** bar for skill and friction (not official). Your mileage varies.
- **Status ~ my *~*:** your **state** and **approximate hours** in one cell, HLTB-style hours (`84h`, `—`). Examples: **`100% ~ 84h`** when done; **`Std ~ —`** when you cleared a bar but did not log time; **`42% ~ 12h`** in progress; leave empty in **To play**.
- **Sections (order):** **100% completed** — you hit *your* completion bar (achievements, HLTB 100%, or a personal goal); **In progress** — actively playing; **To play** — owned, not started or long-term backlog; **To buy** — not owned. Move a **whole row** when status changes (same columns everywhere except **To buy** uses purchase links + **Notes**).
- **To buy:** **purchase / platform** says where to pay; *F2P* means the base download is free but you may still want a controller, currency packs, or editions later.
- **Kind:** short **gameplay** tag (tower defense, find-things, Metroidvania, etc.)—your own labels; not a canonical store genre list.

Links use Steam store pages where available; confirm purchase availability in your region on Steam.

## Notes on difficulty vs 100% time

- **Fighting games (e.g. MK11):** “100%” on HLTB often means **all modes, cosmetics, grind**—huge spread. Story-only is short; completionist is a **different project** than difficulty.
- **Open worlds (Just Cause 3, Unity, Shadow of War):** **100%** is map cleanup and collectibles; **main** is a fairer “how long is the game” anchor.
- **Roguelikes / precision platformers (Spelunky, Super Meat Boy):** **100%** is often **mastery time**, not “content volume”—difficulty and hours decouple.

When a row feels wrong for *your* copy (edition, DLC, platform), override the HLTB columns after checking the exact game name on HLTB.
